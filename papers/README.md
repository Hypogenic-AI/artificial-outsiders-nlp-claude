# Downloaded Papers

## LLM Self-Preference and Evaluation Bias

1. **LLM Evaluators Recognize and Favor Their Own Generations** (panickssery2024_llm_self_preference.pdf)
   - Authors: Panickssery, Bowman, Feng (2024)
   - arXiv: 2404.13076
   - Why relevant: Core finding that LLMs recognize and prefer their own outputs. Linear correlation between self-recognition and self-preference.

2. **Self-Preference Bias in LLM-as-a-Judge** (wataoka2024_self_preference_bias.pdf)
   - Authors: Wataoka, Takahashi, Ri (2024)
   - arXiv: 2410.21819
   - Why relevant: Shows self-preference is driven by perplexity preference---LLMs prefer familiar text. Directly supports our "outsider" hypothesis.

3. **Judging LLM-as-a-judge with MT-Bench and Chatbot Arena** (zheng2023_mt_bench_chatbot_arena.pdf)
   - Authors: Zheng et al. (2023)
   - arXiv: 2306.05685
   - Why relevant: Foundational paper establishing LLM-as-judge methodology and its biases.

4. **CoBBLEr: Benchmarking Cognitive Biases in LLMs as Evaluators** (koo2023_cobbler_cognitive_biases.pdf)
   - Authors: Koo et al. (2023)
   - arXiv: 2309.17012
   - Why relevant: Measures 6 cognitive biases; RBO of 0.44 shows large human-machine preference gap.

5. **Large Language Models are Inconsistent and Biased Evaluators** (stureborg2024_inconsistent_biased.pdf)
   - Authors: Stureborg, Alikaniotis, Suhara (2024)
   - arXiv: 2405.01724
   - Why relevant: Confirms familiarity bias (perplexity preference) in LLM evaluators.

6. **Large Language Models are not Fair Evaluators** (wang2023_not_fair_evaluators.pdf)
   - Authors: Wang et al. (2023)
   - arXiv: 2310.01432
   - Why relevant: Position bias in LLM evaluation; calibration strategies.

7. **Justice or Prejudice? Quantifying Biases in LLM-as-a-Judge** (ye2024_justice_prejudice.pdf)
   - Authors: Ye et al. (2024)
   - arXiv: 2405.12708
   - Why relevant: Systematic framework for 12 bias types in LLM evaluation.

8. **Style Over Substance** (wu2023_style_over_substance.pdf)
   - Authors: Wu, Aji (2023)
   - arXiv: 2307.03025
   - Why relevant: Both humans and LLMs prefer longer, fluent text over factually correct text. LLMs have stronger style bias.

9. **Humans or LLMs as the Judge?** (bavaresco2024_llms_instead_human_judges.pdf)
   - Authors: Bavaresco et al. (2024)
   - arXiv: 2402.10669
   - Why relevant: Compares human and LLM judgment biases.

10. **LLMs as Narcissistic Evaluators** (liu2023_narcissistic_evaluators.pdf)
    - Authors: Liu, Moosavi, Lin (2023)
    - arXiv: 2310.12218
    - Why relevant: Reference-free LLM evaluation metrics favor text from same model.

11. **Play Favorites** (play_favorites_self_bias.pdf)
    - Authors: Spiliopoulou et al. (2025)
    - arXiv: 2404.12272
    - Why relevant: Statistical framework for isolating self-bias from quality differences.

12. **OffsetBias** (offsetbias_debiased_evaluators.pdf)
    - Authors: Park et al. (2024)
    - arXiv: 2408.13128
    - Why relevant: De-biasing dataset and methods for LLM evaluators.

13. **Foundational Autoraters (FLAMe)** (foundational_autoraters.pdf)
    - Authors: Vu et al. (2024)
    - arXiv: 2406.18403
    - Why relevant: Trained evaluator models that are less biased than LLM judges.

14. **No Free Labels** (krumdick2025_no_free_labels.pdf)
    - Authors: Krumdick et al. (2025)
    - arXiv: 2501.12578
    - Why relevant: LLM judges unreliable without human grounding.

15. **Diverging Preferences** (diverging_preferences.pdf)
    - Authors: (2024)
    - arXiv: 2410.14632
    - Why relevant: When annotators disagree and whether models predict disagreement.

## LLM Creativity, Diversity, and Human Preferences

16. **Generative AI Enhances Creativity but Reduces Diversity** (doshi2024_generative_ai_creativity_diversity.pdf)
    - Authors: Doshi, Hauser (2024)
    - arXiv: 2312.00506
    - Why relevant: Core finding: AI access improves individual stories but reduces collective diversity. Central to our hypothesis.

17. **Echoes in AI: Quantifying Lack of Plot Diversity** (echoes_ai_plot_diversity.pdf)
    - Authors: (2024)
    - arXiv: 2501.00273
    - Why relevant: LLM outputs converge on similar narratives even across models. Sui Generis score measures uniqueness.

18. **Art or Artifice? LLMs and the False Promise of Creativity** (chakrabarty2023_art_artifice.pdf)
    - Authors: Chakrabarty et al. (2023)
    - arXiv: 2309.14556
    - Why relevant: Expert analysis of LLM creative writing quality.

19. **A Confederacy of Models** (gomez2023_confederacy_creative_writing.pdf)
    - Authors: Gomez-Rodriguez, Williams (2023)
    - arXiv: 2310.08433
    - Why relevant: Blind human evaluation of creative writing from 13 LLMs.

20. **Can LLMs Generate Novel Research Ideas?** (si2024_novel_research_ideas.pdf)
    - Authors: Si et al. (2024)
    - arXiv: 2409.04109
    - Why relevant: LLM ideas rated as more novel but less feasible by experts.

21. **AI as Humanity's Salieri** (agarwal2024_salieri.pdf)
    - Authors: Agarwal et al. (2024)
    - arXiv: 2410.04265
    - Why relevant: Quantifies linguistic creativity of LLMs vs. web text.

22. **Can AI Writing Be Salvaged?** (can_ai_writing_salvaged.pdf)
    - Authors: (2024)
    - arXiv: 2409.14509
    - Why relevant: How editing improves human-AI alignment in writing.

23. **Is Temperature the Creativity Parameter?** (is_temperature_creativity.pdf)
    - Authors: (2024)
    - arXiv: 2405.00492
    - Why relevant: Temperature effects on LLM output diversity and creativity.

24. **Assessing and Understanding Creativity in LLMs** (chen2024_creativity_llm.pdf)
    - Authors: Chen et al. (2024)
    - arXiv: 2405.00451
    - Why relevant: Framework for evaluating LLM creativity.

25. **AI-generated Poetry Rated More Favorably** (porter2024_ai_poetry_rated_favorably.pdf)
    - Authors: Porter et al. (2024)
    - arXiv: 2310.18502
    - Why relevant: Humans prefer AI poetry over human poetry in blind evaluation.
